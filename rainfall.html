Rainfall analysis of Kenya and Somalia

Spatial analysis of Rainfall distribution using satellite imagery

Details of Rainfall Distribution

The map reveals striking contrasts: Kenya’s highlands and lake regions shimmer with higher rainfall, while Somalia’s landscape remains predominantly arid, with sparse rainfall throughout the year. These patterns reflect not just geography, but the deep link between climate, water access, and community livelihoods across the region. This analysis reminds us that understanding rainfall is more than numbers — it’s about resilience, planning, and adapting to a changing climate.

Methodology

Satellite imagery was processed and classified to identify vegetation, water bodies, and built-up areas.

Tools Used

ArcGIS Pro CHIRPS Dataset from Digital Earth Africa
