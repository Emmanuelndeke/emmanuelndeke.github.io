Rainfall analysis of Kenya and Somalia

The map reveals striking contrasts: Kenya’s highlands and lake regions shimmer with higher rainfall, while Somalia’s landscape remains predominantly arid, with sparse rainfall throughout the year. These patterns reflect not just geography, but the deep link between climate, water access, and community livelihoods across the region. This analysis reminds us that understanding rainfall is more than numbers — it’s about resilience, planning, and adapting to a changing climate.

Methodology

Rainfall distribution was analyzed for Kenya and Somalia using precipitation data from the CHIRPS dataset obtained through Digital Earth Africa. The dataset covered the period from January to August 2025 and was processed in ArcGIS Pro. The rainfall rasters were first imported and clipped to the combined boundaries of Kenya and Somalia in order to focus the analysis on the study area. To understand rainfall patterns during the study period, the monthly datasets were processed to calculate the average rainfall across the region. The resulting raster was then symbolized using a graduated color scheme to show variations in rainfall intensity, allowing wetter and drier areas to be clearly identified. The final map was arranged in a layout including essential cartographic elements such as a title, legend, scale bar, and data source information to clearly communicate rainfall patterns across the two countries.

Tools Used

  • ArcGIS Pro
  • CHIRPS Dataset from Digital Earth Africa
