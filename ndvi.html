Vegetation Health Classification

This map tells an unexpected story. While Lake Chad is known for its long-term decline, my NDVI analysis reveals a different trend between 2017 and 2024. The 2024 data shows noticeable greening and a more consolidated water body compared to 2017. This doesn't solve the long-term crisis for the millions in Chad, Nigeria, Niger, and Cameroon who depend on it, but it highlights the lake's resilience and complexity.

Methodology

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) analysis was carried out for the Lake Chad region using imagery from the Landsat USGS Earth Explorer Program. The satellite data were processed in QGIS to examine vegetation conditions around the lake. Two band combinations were tested for comparison. The standard NDVI calculation used Band 5 (near-infrared) and Band 4 (red) to measure vegetation health and density. An additional comparison was performed using Band 4 and Band 3, allowing observation of how different spectral band combinations influence vegetation detection. The NDVI outputs were then visualized using a color ramp to distinguish areas with dense vegetation, sparse vegetation, and non-vegetated surfaces. This comparison helped demonstrate the effectiveness of near-infrared data in identifying vegetation patterns around the Lake Chad basin.

Tools Used

  • QGIS Software
  • Landsat Band 4&3 and Band 5&4 collections from USGS Earth Explorer
