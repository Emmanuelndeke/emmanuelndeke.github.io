Land Cover Classification

The analysis shows that built-up areas dominate the city, covering about 62% of the total area, while rangelands account for 26%. Vegetation cover, including trees and grasslands, occupies a smaller proportion, and water bodies represent only a minimal share. The results highlight the strong urban footprint of Nairobi and provide a spatial overview of how different land cover types are distributed across the city.

Methodology

Land cover classification for Kenya was developed using a global land cover dataset processed in QGIS. The raster dataset was first imported and clipped to the national boundary of Kenya using an administrative boundary shapefile to ensure the analysis focused only on the study area. The land cover layer contained multiple classes representing different surface types such as forest, cropland, grassland, built-up areas, water bodies, and bare land. After preparing the dataset, categorized symbology was applied to visually distinguish each land cover class using appropriate colors to improve map readability. To further analyze the spatial distribution of land cover types, zonal statistics were used to calculate the area covered by each class. The resulting values were exported and organized in Microsoft Excel, where additional analysis was carried out. Pie charts and bar charts were created to clearly illustrate the proportion and comparison of different land cover categories across Kenya. These visualizations helped to summarize the land cover composition and provide an easy interpretation of dominant land use patterns. The final cartographic layout included essential map elements such as the title, legend, scale bar, north arrow, and data source information to produce a clear and informative land cover map.

Tools Used

  • QGIS Software
  • Microsoft Excel
  • Esri 10 m Global Land Cover dataset (2024) and DEM elevation data from USGS Earth Explorer.
