Population Density of Kenya

I recently created this population density map of Kenya using the Global Human Settlement Population Grid (GHS-POP) dataset. The map visualizes population counts per 100-meter grid cell, revealing how people are distributed across the country. The visualization highlights strong population concentration in major urban and agricultural regions such as Nairobi, the Lake Victoria basin around Kisumu, and the central highlands. In contrast, northern counties show much lower population densities, reflecting the environmental and socio-economic differences across Kenya.

Methodology

Population distribution for Kenya was mapped using the Global Human Settlement Population Grid produced by the European Commission Joint Research Centre. The population raster dataset was imported into QGIS and clipped to the national boundary of Kenya using a Kenya administrative boundary shapefile to restrict the analysis to the study area. The raster values represent the estimated number of people per 100-metre grid cell. After clipping the dataset, NoData values were standardized to improve visualization and ensure that only valid population cells were displayed. The raster layer was then symbolized using a Singleband Pseudocolor renderer with a graduated color ramp ranging from black to white to highlight variations in population density. Areas with low population appear darker while densely populated regions appear brighter, allowing spatial patterns of settlement distribution across Kenya to be clearly visualized. The final map layout was composed with essential cartographic elements including a title, legend, scale bar, data source attribution, and map credits.
